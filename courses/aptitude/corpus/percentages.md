# Percentages

## Core Formula
Percentage = (Part / Whole) × 100

Example: 45 out of 180 = (45/180) × 100 = 25%.

## Percentage Increase and Decrease
% Increase = (Increase / Original) × 100
% Decrease = (Decrease / Original) × 100

Price goes from 80 to 100: % increase = (20/80) × 100 = 25%.

## Successive Percentage Changes
Two successive changes of a% and b% result in a net change of: a + b + (ab/100) %.

Example: 20% increase then 10% decrease = 20 + (−10) + (20×−10/100) = 10 − 2 = 8% net increase.

## Profit and Loss
Profit % = (Profit / Cost Price) × 100
Loss % = (Loss / Cost Price) × 100
Selling Price = Cost Price × (1 + Profit%/100)

If CP = 200 and SP = 250: Profit = 50, Profit% = 25%.

## Simple Interest
SI = (Principal × Rate × Time) / 100
Amount = Principal + SI

P = 5000, R = 8%, T = 3 years: SI = 5000×8×3/100 = 1200. Amount = 6200.

## Compound Interest
CI = P × (1 + R/100)^T − P

P = 1000, R = 10%, T = 2 years: Amount = 1000 × 1.1^2 = 1210. CI = 210.
