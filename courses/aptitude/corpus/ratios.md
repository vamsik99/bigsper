# Ratios and Proportion

## Ratio Basics
A ratio a:b means for every a parts of one quantity there are b parts of another.
Ratios can be simplified: 12:16 = 3:4.

## Proportion
a:b = c:d means a×d = b×c (cross-multiplication).

Example: If 6:x = 3:7, then x = 6×7/3 = 14.

## Dividing in a Ratio
To divide quantity Q in ratio a:b:
- First part = Q × a/(a+b)
- Second part = Q × b/(a+b)

Divide 480 in ratio 3:5: parts = 480×3/8 = 180, and 480×5/8 = 300.

## Compound Ratios
Compound ratio of a:b and c:d is ac:bd.

Example: compound ratio of 2:3 and 4:5 = 8:15.

## Direct and Inverse Variation
Direct variation: if x increases, y increases proportionally. x/y = constant.
Inverse variation: if x increases, y decreases. x×y = constant.

5 workers complete a job in 12 days. How long for 4 workers? (inverse: 5×12 = 4×d, d = 15 days.)

## Mixtures and Alligation
To find the ratio in which two items at prices A and B must be mixed to get mean price M:
Ratio = (B − M) : (M − A)

Mix milk at Rs 16/L and water (Rs 0) to get mixture at Rs 12/L:
Ratio = (16−12):(12−0) = 4:12 = 1:3. So 1 part milk : 3 parts water.
