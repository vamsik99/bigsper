# Time, Speed and Distance

## Core Formula
Speed = Distance / Time
Distance = Speed × Time
Time = Distance / Speed

Units: km/h or m/s. To convert km/h → m/s: multiply by 5/18.
Example: 72 km/h = 72 × 5/18 = 20 m/s.

## Average Speed
When same distance is covered at two speeds u and v:
Average speed = 2uv / (u + v)  (harmonic mean — NOT arithmetic mean)

Example: 60 km at 30 km/h and 60 km at 60 km/h.
Average speed = 2×30×60/(30+60) = 3600/90 = 40 km/h.

## Relative Speed
Two objects moving in the SAME direction: relative speed = |v₁ − v₂|
Two objects moving in OPPOSITE directions: relative speed = v₁ + v₂

## Trains
Time to cross a stationary pole/person = Length of train / Speed of train
Time to cross a platform = (Length of train + Length of platform) / Speed of train
Two trains passing each other = Sum of lengths / Relative speed

Example: Train 200 m long at 54 km/h (= 15 m/s) crosses a 100 m platform.
Time = (200 + 100) / 15 = 300/15 = 20 seconds.

## Boats and Streams
Speed downstream = Boat speed + Stream speed
Speed upstream = Boat speed − Stream speed
Boat speed = (downstream + upstream) / 2
Stream speed = (downstream − upstream) / 2
